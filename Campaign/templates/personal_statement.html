Change

             Without a doubt, we are all susceptible to change. According to some, the very definition of learning is to change. Over the past three years the school has changed, we’ve seen introductions of clubs, innovative changes to senate processes, and we’ve even managed to have a new teacher every year that we’ve been here.

Now, on the verge of a great change within ourselves, we must ask: what is left to do? I don’t believe in a single person knowing the answer. We, the seniors, being the leaders in our school, must decide that together, before we miss the opportunity to leave the school in an acceptable condition for future generations. Electing our senators is a start, but even representatives may fail to hear the unheard, the reserved introverts, the ones who either out of fear, time constraints, or a number of other reasons aren’t able to participate in senate. What we need is an open communication channel between the senate and the student body. A mutually beneficial relationship where the Senate is open and communicative with the student body and in turn the student body involves itself with senate matters.

This channel must be accessible, understandable, and easily digestible so that the involved student no longer needs to dedicate hours preparing a senate presentation or attempting to indirectly get their voices heard though a 3rd party senator, rather, directly stating what they mean so the senate is able to develop, adapt, and deploy changes to an ever-changing student body. The senate in return may provide the meeting notes publicly so that involved and concerned students can ensure they are getting through. Through this methodology, the senate is guaranteed improvement and better representation over time. This is my goal with the Senate, I want to build an open communication channel. The website, in its current state, is a glimpse into what I believe this school’s future is. A connected student body, one where each student has a stock in their school, communicating through modern tools with a dynamic and adaptable senate.
